# Office Manager Handoff — Adam → Carey

The responsibilities Carey takes over as office manager, plus the one stream
that stays with Adam. Everything here is done in **Construction Lodestar**
(https://utility-workbench.vercel.app) on any device she's signed in on —
phone, Windows laptop, anywhere.

> Each section below points to its in-app **📖 Guide** (More ▾ menu → 📖 Guide).
> Those guides are the step-by-step "how to" written for exactly this handoff —
> Carey can follow them with no prior knowledge. Pull them up live during
> training week.

---

## Day 1 checklist

1. Open the invite email (Adam creates the login in 👥 People) and set your password.
2. Sign in at https://utility-workbench.vercel.app — bookmark it on every device you'll use.
3. Open the **More ▾** menu → **📖 Guide** and skim the list. This is your manual — every job below has a guide.
4. Go to **✓ Tasks** and set the **Show** filter to **My queue**. That's your list: tasks assigned to you, plus anything unassigned.
5. Glance at **🏠 Today** — if unassigned tasks are waiting, the header shows a count "up for grabs."
6. Capture a practice order: type a line like "almond slab" into the **Quick add** bar at the top of the Projects tab, see it land on the matched house's 🛒 Materials, then delete it there.
7. Open any house and click the **⚙️ gear** in its header — that's where a house's facts live (electric utility, water source, Duke office, closing date).

## What Carey takes over

### 1. ⚡ Electric — determine the utility provider for each lot
Before any application can go out, Carey figures out **which electric utility
serves the lot** based on its location / service territory, and records it on
the house: open the **⚙️ gear** in the house header and set the **Utility**
field. This drives everything downstream — which application flow, which
contacts, where every email routes.

The providers we run into:
- **SECO** — built-in application flow (see below). Phone 352-569-9594.
- **Duke Energy** — built-in flow. Phone 352-239-5698. Duke houses also need
  the **Duke EDA office** picked in the same ⚙️ gear — it's deliberately blank
  until you choose (most Marion County jobs → **Ocala**; western / Citrus side
  → **Inverness**). Every Duke email for the house routes to that office, so
  the app warns until it's set. Pick whichever office emails the Work Order.
- **Clay Electric** — **phone-based**, no built-in application flow.
  Apply by phone: 1-800-224-4917. Record the utility as Clay so the house
  doesn't show a SECO/Duke flow that doesn't apply.
- **City of Ocala Electric (or any other provider)** — flag it to Adam. He can
  add the company under Settings → Utility companies setup, which gives the
  house 📞 call / ✉️ email buttons (like Clay — no auto-filled application).
  Then handle the application by phone / that utility's own process.

> ⚠️ Getting the provider right is the whole game — a wrong utility means the
> wrong application, wrong contacts, and a silent stall. When in doubt, confirm
> with the utility by phone before applying.

### 2. ⚡ Electric — new service applications
Once the provider is known, get power applied for. Both built-in flows run from
**Batch apply** on the Projects screen.
- **SECO houses** (email-first): download the pre-filled load form, tick the
  three boxes + sign, draft the email, attach the load form + site plan, send,
  then ✓ Mark applied. → Guide: **"Apply for SECO electric."**
- **Duke houses** (portal-first): apply on the Duke Builder Portal, wait for
  the Work Order # email, record the WO# and EDA office in the ⚙️ gear, then
  send the pre-filled load form back through Batch apply (keep "WO#…" in the
  subject). → Guide: **"Apply for Duke electric."**
- **Clay / City of Ocala / other**: handle by phone / that utility's own
  process (no app flow), as noted above.
- If a row shows a yellow ⚠ warning (e.g. legal description needs lookup), fix
  it first — that field will be blank on the downloaded form.

### 3. 📸 Electric — notify the utility the home is ready for the meter
When a house passes its electrical inspection (green tag), click the 📸
"Notify utility — ready for meter" button on the Electric tab. The email opens
already addressed to the right place — SECO Engineering, or the house's Duke
EDA office — with the photo checklist (green tag, downpipe, sweep, straps,
clear path). → Guide: **"Tell the utility a home is ready for the meter."**
- ⚠️ The email *lists* the photos — Carey must attach the actual pictures, or
  the utility won't schedule the meter set.

### 4. 💧 Water — set up water on each house
Well, city water, or city water needing a main extension.
- **First** set the Water source in the house's **⚙️ gear** (Well / City /
  City + main extension) — the checklist changes with the source, and a house
  with no source set shows no checklist at all. Well = check it off once
  drilled. City = confirm availability with **Marion County Utilities (MCU)**
  and apply. City + main extension = the main gets extended first.
  → Guide: **"Set up water for a house."**

### 5. 🚽 Septic / Sewer — set up waste handling
- **Septic**: our provider is **Georges Plumbing & Excavating (Vicki Kirby)** —
  site/soil eval → DEP construction permit → installed → notify Vicki when the
  water line is hooked up and when sod is laid. (INRB systems add one extra
  step — a recorded notice to Georges; the tab flags it.)
- **Sewer**: confirm availability with MCU, apply, pay tap/connection fees.
- ⚠️ The #1 mistake: a sewer lot left set to "Septic" (the default) starts a
  DEP permit you don't need — confirm the source in the ⚙️ gear first.
  → Guide: **"Set up septic (or sewer) for a house."**

### 6. 🛒 Materials & Orders — the flagship job
Capturing what the field needs and getting it ordered.
- **Capture from Josh's texts**: type or paste his lines into the **Quick add**
  bar at the top of the Projects tab — it matches each line to the right house
  + material, files it as "To order," and won't let you double-order. Works
  from any device you're signed in on. (Adam's office Mac has an automatic
  "Scan Josh" button that reads his texts directly — that's Adam's convenience
  only; Quick add is how everyone else captures orders.) → Guide:
  **"Capture orders from Josh's texts."**
- **Place the order**: on a "To order" row, click **✉️ Order from [vendor]** —
  the email is fully written and addressed; read it and send. Or 📞 to call.
  Then move the row To order → Ordered → Delivered/Installed and set the date.
  → Guide: **"Order materials / reach a vendor."**
- **Vendors directory** (More ▾ menu → 🚚 Vendors) for any supplier's number or
  a blank email.

### 7. 🏠 Run the day — the Today screen
First thing each morning and whenever she sits back down.
- Work it **top-down**: ⭐ Today's focus → 🔥 Needs attention (real deadlines:
  due-soon tasks, **electric shut-offs**) → ⚠ Gone quiet (houses stalled at a
  stage) → ⏳ Waiting on you → ✅ Ready for your move.
  → Guide: **"Run your day (the Today screen)."**

### 8. ✅ Cross-project tasks
The ✓ Tasks tab — capturing, assigning, starring, and clearing the day-to-day
to-dos that aren't tied to one checklist step.
- Every task can be **assigned to a person**. The **Show** filter switches
  between **My queue** (yours + anything unassigned), **Unassigned**, each
  person by name, and **Everyone**.
- New tasks default to assigned-to-you; leave the assignee blank to put one in
  the shared pile. Today's header counts unassigned tasks "up for grabs" so
  the pile never hides.

### 9. 🔍 Inspections
Tracking inspection results per house (the 🔍 Inspections tab). Note this is
separate from permits — inspection *results* live here, not in the Permit
stream.

### 10. ⚡ Electric shut-offs after a sale
When a house sells, the electric account should be shut off / transferred
**2 business days after closing**. Set the closing date in the house's ⚙️
gear; the Today screen flags the deadline in 🔥 Needs attention so it doesn't
get missed.

### 11. 📧 Office email & bookkeeping intake
Maintain the office inbox (`office@ironshieldconstruction.com`) day-to-day, and
forward **invoices and receipts** to **`data@ironshieldconstruction.com`** so
they reach bookkeeping.
- **Invoices**: forward as-is.
- **Receipts**: when forwarding, the email must specify **(1) what it was for**
  if it isn't obvious from the receipt, and **(2) which card was used** for the
  purchase. A receipt with no purpose and no card can't be reconciled, so this
  is required, not optional.

### 12. 📦 Office supplies & inventory
Keep an eye on office stock and reorder before it runs out — both **regular
office supplies** (paper, ink, pens, etc.) and **coffee / food / kitchen**
items. Restock proactively so nothing runs dry.

## If something looks wrong

**Ask Adam.** A house showing the wrong utility, an email addressed somewhere
unexpected, a checklist that doesn't match reality, a login or screen that
looks off — flag it before acting, don't work around it.

And the one rule: **never guess on permits.** Anything touching the 📋 Permit
stream — new permits, the Jennifer handoff, permit expiry, county questions —
is Adam's, full stop. Hand it to him and move on to the next thing.
